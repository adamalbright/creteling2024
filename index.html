Background readings (link to file on CreteLing website)

  • If you have no prior experience with Optimality Theory, Kager (1999) chapter 1 is a good brief introduction, and McCarthy (2008), chapter 1 goes into a bit more detail. 
  • I assigned Kenstowicz and Kisseberth (1977), chapter 1, but I later realized that it makes sense to talk about some other preliminaries first, before we dig into that!  

Additional readings:

  • Becker, M., Ketrez, N., and Nevins, A. (2011). The surfeit of the stimulus: Analytic biases filter lexical statistics in Turkish laryngeal alternations. Language, 87:84–125.
  • Chapter 8 of Chomsky, N. and Halle, M. (1968). The sound pattern of English. Harper and Row, New York.
  • Hayes, B. (1995). On what to teach the undergraduates: Some changing orthodoxies in phonological theory. In Lee, I.-H., editor, Linguistics in the Morning Calm, volume 3, pages 59–77. Hanshin, Seoul.
  • Hayes (1999) Phonological Restructuring in Yidiɲ.
